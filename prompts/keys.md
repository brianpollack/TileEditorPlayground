This guide defines the visual principles for creating assets that fit a cohesive, stylized, top-down game world. It prioritizes readability, warmth, and simplicity over realism.

## 1. Core Style Principles

### 1.1 Readability First
- All assets must be **clearly identifiable at a distance**
- Avoid fine detail that disappears when zoomed out
- Favor **strong silhouettes** over internal detail

### 1.2 Stylization Over Realism
- Do not aim for photorealism
- Use **simplified geometry and exaggerated proportions**
- Think “toy-like” or “storybook” rather than real-world scale

## 2. Color System

### 2.1 Palette Structure
- Use **warm, saturated, and inviting colors**
- Favor **analogous palettes** (e.g., green → yellow → orange)
- Avoid harsh color clashes unless for focal points

### 2.2 Environmental Colors
- Terrain: Soft, vibrant greens
- Objects: Warm browns, muted reds, soft neutrals
- Accents: Yellow highlights for warmth and light

### 2.3 Contrast Rules
- **Global contrast:** Low (soft, cohesive world)
- **Local contrast:** High at focal points (e.g., buildings, interactables)

### 2.4 Hue Shifting
- Shadows should **shift hue slightly cooler**, not just darker
- Highlights can shift slightly warmer

---

## 3. Lighting & Shading

### 3.1 Light Direction
- Primary light source: **Top-left**
- Maintain consistency across all assets

### 3.2 Shadow Style
- Soft-edged shadows
- Slightly desaturated and cooler than base color
- Avoid pure black shadows

### 3.3 Shading Approach
- Use **soft gradients**, not hard cel shading
- Avoid sharp lighting transitions
- Emphasize **volume through subtle shading**

### 3.4 Ambient Occlusion (AO)
- Light AO should be implied:
  - Under objects
  - At contact points (tree bases, walls)
- Keep it soft and subtle

---

## 4. Geometry & Form

### 4.1 Shape Language
- Prefer **rounded, soft forms**
- Avoid sharp, aggressive edges
- Use simple primitives (spheres, cylinders, blocks)

### 4.2 Proportions
- Slightly exaggerated proportions improve readability
- Keep assets **visually balanced and compact**

### 4.3 Detail Level
- Low to medium detail
- Focus detail where it supports recognition

---

## 5. Texturing & Materials

### 5.1 Texture Style
- Use **hand-painted textures**
- Avoid high-frequency noise or realism-heavy detail

### 5.2 Material Response
- Minimal reliance on:
  - Roughness maps
  - Normal maps
- Let **color and shading drive form**

### 5.3 Surface Treatment
- Smooth, clean surfaces
- Subtle variation to avoid flatness

---

## 6. Composition & Placement

### 6.1 Visual Hierarchy
- Key objects (e.g., buildings) should:
  - Use higher contrast
  - Have clearer shapes
  - Be slightly more detailed

### 6.2 Repetition & Rhythm
- Repeat elements (trees, stones) to create flow
- Introduce small variations to avoid monotony

### 6.3 Balance
- Distribute objects organically
- Avoid perfect symmetry
- Maintain visual weight across the scene

---

## 7. Environment Design

### 7.1 Terrain
- Smooth gradients, minimal texture noise
- Avoid clutter

### 7.2 Vegetation
- Trees: Rounded clusters (“lollipop” style)
- Bushes: Simple, soft forms
- Keep silhouettes distinct

### 7.3 Paths & Structures
- Slightly stylized and uniform
- Clean edges, consistent spacing
- Avoid irregular realism

---

## 8. Technical Guidelines

### 8.1 Rendering Approach
- Compatible with:
  - Hand-painted pipelines
  - Lightweight PBR (optional)
- Avoid heavy shader complexity

### 8.2 Performance Considerations
- Optimize for:
  - Low poly counts
  - Minimal texture memory
- Design for scalability (mobile → desktop)

---

## 9. Do / Don’t Summary

### Do
- Use soft lighting and gradients
- Keep shapes simple and readable
- Maintain consistent light direction
- Use warm, harmonious colors

### Don’t
- Use photorealistic textures
- Add unnecessary fine detail
- Use harsh shadows or pure black
- Break color harmony

---

## 10. Style Keywords
Use these to guide asset creation:

- Stylized
- Soft
- Warm
- Readable
- Simplified
- Hand-painted
- Playful
- Clean

---

## 11. Reference Style Alignment
Assets should feel consistent with:
- Casual simulation / farming games
- Stylized mobile environments
- Hand-painted 3D worlds

---

## Summary
This style is defined by:
- **Clarity over complexity**
- **Warmth over realism**
- **Consistency over variation**

Every asset should contribute to a world that is:
Easy to read, pleasant to explore, and visually cohesive.
